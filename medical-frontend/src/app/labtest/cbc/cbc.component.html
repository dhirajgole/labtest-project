Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Measures different components of blood to assess overall health

What is a CBC Test?

A Complete Blood Count (CBC) test is a common blood test that evaluates the overall health of an individual by measuring the levels of red blood cells, white blood cells, hemoglobin, and platelets. It helps in detecting infections, anemia, immune system disorders, and other medical conditions.

Why is this test prescribed?

  • To check for anemia and infections
  • To investigate symptoms like fatigue, fever, weakness, or bruising
  • To monitor chronic conditions and ongoing treatments
  • As part of routine health checkups

Key Parameters Included

  • Hemoglobin (Hb): Measures oxygen-carrying capacity of blood
  • Red Blood Cells (RBC): Indicates oxygen transport efficiency
  • White Blood Cells (WBC): Helps detect infections and immune response
  • Platelet Count: Assesses blood clotting ability
  • Hematocrit (HCT): Percentage of RBCs in blood

Who should take this test?

  • People experiencing weakness, dizziness, or tiredness
  • Patients with frequent infections or fever
  • Individuals with unexplained weight loss
  • People undergoing surgery or medical treatment

Test Preparation

No special preparation or fasting is required for the CBC test. However, inform your doctor about any medications you are taking.

Sample & Report Details

  • Sample Type: Blood sample
  • Fasting Required: No
  • Report Time: Same day or within 24 hours

Note: Abnormal CBC values may indicate infections, nutritional deficiencies, blood disorders, or chronic diseases. Always consult a doctor for interpretation.
